# AI in Digital Humanities: Projects, Grants, Successes & Risks (2025–2026)
**Prepared for the Connecticut Digital Humanities Conference — March 2026**

## Executive Summary
Artificial intelligence has transitioned from an experimental auxiliary to a core methodological tool across the digital humanities landscape. The period from January 2025 through March 2026 has been defined by major grant investments—most notably **Schmidt Sciences’ $11 million HAVI program**—and landmark breakthroughs in manuscript recovery and archival access.

## I. The Major Funding Landscape (2025–2026)
### Schmidt Sciences: HAVI Program
* **Total Investment**: $11 million awarded to 23 interdisciplinary teams in December 2025.
* **Next Deadline**: March 13, 2026.

### National Endowment for the Humanities (NEH)
* **Activity**: Awarded $22.6 million for 219 projects in early 2025.
* **Current Status**: The Digital Humanities Advancement Grants program is cancelled for FY2026.

## II. Manuscript Transcription (HTR)
* **CoMMA Project**: Transcribed 32,763 medieval manuscripts in four months (Jan 2026).
* **Transkribus**: Dominant platform; avoids "hallucinations" common in LLMs.

## III. Ancient Text Recovery & Archaeology
* **Aeneas (DeepMind)**: Dates Latin inscriptions within a decade.
* **Vesuvius Challenge**: Revealed Greek text from 2,000-year-old carbonized scrolls.

## IV. Social Justice & Underrepresented Communities
* **Communities in the Loop**: AI tools identifying "veiled protest" in 19th-century Black newspapers.
* **Indigenous Language**: FLAIR Initiative and Skobot for language revitalization.

## V. Ethical Risks & Critical Perspectives
* **Training Data Bias**: Leads to "cultural flattening."
* **The Scraping Crisis**: AI bots systematically scraping GLAM (Galleries, Libraries, Archives, Museums) collections regardless of robots.txt.
